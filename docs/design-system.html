PriceLens ◍ · Design System

Tatami

A measured, Muji-minimalist system · Sistem desain yang tenang & terukur

Tatami is the visual language of PriceLens. Like the tatami mat that quietly sets the proportions of a Japanese room, it gives every screen a calm grid, generous negative space, and a single warm accent. The goal: let the numbers speak, and remove everything that competes with them.

Principles · Prinsip

Five quiet rules

Kanso — simplicity. Remove the non-essential. One idea per section, one accent colour, no decoration that doesn't carry meaning. Hapus yang tidak perlu.
Ma — negative space. Whitespace is a feature. Spacing uses an 8 px rhythm so the eye can rest between data. Ruang kosong sebagai elemen.
Shizen — naturalness. Warm, paper-like neutrals over harsh white; nothing shouts. Nuansa hangat, tidak menyilaukan.
Seijaku — stillness. Calm motion, soft hovers, no flashing. Tables and charts feel composed, not busy. Tenang, tanpa kebisingan visual.
Kaizen — refinement. The system ships small and improves continuously; tokens make change cheap. Perbaikan berkelanjutan.

Voice & tone · Suara

How PriceLens speaks

Do
  • Plain, precise, bilingual (EN primary, ID caption).
  • Lead with the number, then the nuance.
  • Always offer a way to verify (listing link).
Don't
  • Hype, exclamation marks, or false precision.
  • Hide assumptions behind a “fair price”.
  • Claim live data when showing a snapshot.
