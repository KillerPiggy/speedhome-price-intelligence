01 · Chief Executive — Strategy

Why this matters

A rental marketplace lives or dies on pricing confidence. Owners over-ask and units sit empty; they under-ask and leave money on the table; tenants can’t tell a fair deal from a bad one. PriceLens turns a noisy page of listings into a single, defensible answer: “what is the fair monthly rent here, and why?” Pasar sewa hidup dari kepercayaan harga.

The bet · Taruhan strategis

  • For the business: faster, more accurate listing pricing → shorter days-on-market → more closed tenancies.
  • For owners & agents: a “fair price” they can show a client, not just an opinion.
  • For tenants: transparency that builds trust in the platform.

“Make the invisible obvious. A good price tool removes an argument, not just a number.”

03 · Engineering — Architecture

How it fits together

Design decisions

  • JSON-first extraction. SPEEDHOME is JS-rendered, so we parse the page’s own state JSON (__NEXT_DATA__/JSON-LD) before falling back to HTML cards — far more stable than CSS selectors.
  • One normalised schema. Every strategy funnels through normalize_listing(); the UI only ever sees clean rows.
  • Honest fallback. If the host blocks the server IP (common on free clouds, and explicitly allowed by the brief), we serve a committed snapshot and say so in the status bar.
  • Politeness by default. robots.txt is checked; requests are spaced; the User-Agent is descriptive; pages per run are capped.
  • Caching. st.cache_data avoids re-hitting the network for repeated queries.

How each statistic is defined · Definisi statistik

  • Average — arithmetic mean of monthly rent in the segment.
  • Median — middle value; robust to a few luxury outliers.
  • Mode — most frequent price; on small samples, the most common RM100 bucket.
  • Fair price — outlier-trimmed mean (drop top/bottom 10%), rounded to RM50; median if <5 units. A defensible “middle” that ignores penthouses and fire-sales.
  • Method note — daily short-stays are excluded from monthly stats (they’d inflate them) but still reported under coverage.

Integrity: the committed snapshot (data/speedhome_sample.json, 62 units, dated 2026-06-20) is the data of record for offline mode; live mode captures the same fields directly.

05 · UI/UX Designer

Calm by design

The interface follows the Tatami design system — washi-paper neutrals, sumi-ink text, one clay accent, and an 8 px spacing rhythm (ma). Numbers are set in a serif (Spectral) for quiet authority; everything functional uses Inter.

  • One screen, top-to-bottom story: search → summary → visuals → insights → listings → export.
  • Mobile-first behaviours: tables scroll horizontally, cards stack, padding tightens under 640 px.
  • Accessibility: AA contrast; accent never used for small body text; keyboard-navigable autocomplete in the demo.
  • Honesty cues: a status bar always states live vs snapshot, robots status, and unit count.
06 · SEO Specialist

Findable, shareable

Because the deliverable is a public web app, it should also be legible to search engines and link unfurls.

  • Meta & titles: descriptive <title> + meta description on the demo & docs.
  • Open Graph / Twitter cards (recommended for deploy): title, description, preview image of the summary table.
  • Structured data: ingesting SPEEDHOME’s JSON-LD also positions us to emit our own Dataset schema for the price summary.
  • Keyword framing: “Mont Kiara rental price”, “KLCC fair rent”, “SPEEDHOME price check”, “sewa Mont Kiara harga wajar”.
  • Performance = SEO: lean deps, cached fetches, no heavy hero media → fast LCP, good Core Web Vitals.

09 · E-commerce Officer

From data to conversion

Treat each listing like a product card and each area like a category page. The funnel: discover (search/autocomplete) → compare (summary, filters, compare-areas) → decide (fair price + ROI) → act (click out / export).

  • Merchandising: surface “best value (RM/sqft)” to mimic a bestseller badge.
  • Conversion aids: ROI/yield calculator answers the buyer’s real question — “is this worth it?”.
  • Export = lead magnet: the dated spreadsheet is something users keep and share.
  • Trust signals: Zero-Deposit flag, verifiable links, honest data-source badge.
10 · Account Manager

Using PriceLens with a client

  • Pre-meeting: export the area sheet, walk in with the fair-price range, not a guess.
  • Repricing conversations: show median + spread to justify a rent adjustment — “your unit is 12% above the area median”.
  • Retention: a quarterly “your area, your fair rent” report keeps owners engaged with the platform.
  • Bilingual deliverables reduce friction with both KL and Indonesian owners.
11 · Unit Manager — Operations

Pricing a unit, day to day

  • List a new unit: match it to its segment (e.g. 2BR, ~1,100 sqft) and price at the fair-price ± furnishing premium.
  • Furnishing lever: the insight “fully-furnished rents ~X% higher” informs whether to furnish before listing.
  • Occupancy vs yield: price toward the median to fill fast; toward the upper quartile to maximise yield on a strong unit.
  • Portfolio view: compare-areas mode helps decide where to acquire or push inventory.
